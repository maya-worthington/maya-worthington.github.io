What is GIScience?

Geographic Information Science (GIScience) is the academic field that studies how we collect, represent, analyze, and understand spatial data. It’s more than just using GIS software, it’s about understanding the science behind the systems we use to map the world, make decisions, and interpret spatial patterns.

What is GIS?

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are the tools and technologies used to store, manage, analyze, and visualize spatial data. A GIS integrates multiple layers of data (elevation, land use, transportation networks, and population density) using spatial relationships like proximity, connectivity, and overlay. These tools help researchers and decision-makers visualize patterns, evaluate accessibility, and support spatial planning in a wide range of fields.

The power of GIS lies in its ability to solve multidimensional problems by integrating spatial information into decision-making. For instance, in public health, GIS has been used to track the spread of infectious diseases such as COVID-19 by mapping infection rates against population density and mobility patterns. In environmental science, GIS plays a central role in monitoring deforestation by analysing satellite imagery to detect land cover change over time. GIS is not just about visualising space, it’s about critically analysing how space functions in relation to social and ecological systems.
